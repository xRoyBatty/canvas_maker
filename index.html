üìñ PROCESS DESCRIPTION (Read before labeling):

The blown film extrusion process begins when resin pellets (plastic granules) are fed into a funnel-shaped container called the hopper. From there, the pellets enter the extruder, a horizontal machine where they are melted and mixed by a rotating screw. At the end of the extruder, the molten plastic passes through the die - a circular opening that shapes the plastic into a tube. An air ring surrounding the die blows air into the center, inflating the plastic into a bubble (like blowing up a balloon). As the bubble rises vertically through the machine, it is guided by a triangular metal frame called the collapsing frame. At the top, two nip rolls (rollers) press the bubble flat, transforming it from a tube into a double-layer flat film called layflat. This flattened film then passes over several idler rolls (guide rollers) before being wound onto a large spool, creating a roll of film ready for further processing.
